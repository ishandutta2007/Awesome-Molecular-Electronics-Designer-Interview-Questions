# Additional Resources

A short list of resources for going deeper on molecular electronics. (Not exhaustive — PRs welcome.) This field sits at the intersection of physical chemistry, condensed matter/device physics, and electrical engineering — cross-check any specific conductance value, material claim, or technology-readiness claim against current, peer-reviewed literature before an interview.

## Foundational Reading
- Cuevas & Scheer, *Molecular Electronics: An Introduction to Theory and Experiment* — a comprehensive, technically rigorous textbook covering both the transport theory and experimental technique fundamentals discussed throughout this repo.
- Nitzan & Ratner, "Electron Transport in Molecular Wire Junctions" (Science, 2003) — a widely-cited, accessible review of the field's core transport physics concepts.
- Aviram & Ratner, "Molecular rectifiers" (Chemical Physics Letters, 1974) — the original conceptual proposal discussed in section 3, useful historical and conceptual grounding.
- Xu & Tao, "Measurement of Single-Molecule Resistance by Repeated Formation of Molecular Junctions" (Science, 2003) — a foundational paper on the STM break-junction statistical measurement approach discussed in section 4.

## Key Journals & Venues (good to be conversationally familiar with)
- *Nature Electronics*, *Nature Nanotechnology*, and *Nano Letters* frequently publish molecular electronics research and are good sources for current state-of-the-art results.
- The Journal of the American Chemical Society and Angewandte Chemie for the synthetic chemistry side of new molecular candidate design.
- Major condensed matter and nanoscience conferences (e.g., APS March Meeting sessions on molecular electronics) for a sense of current research directions.

## Tools (good to have working familiarity with, depending on role focus)
- A quantum chemistry/DFT software package commonly used for molecular electronic structure calculations.
- A quantum transport calculation package implementing the DFT+NEGF approach discussed in section 5.
- Familiarity with break-junction and SAM-based measurement setups if pursuing an experimentally-focused role.

## Communities & Discussion
- r/chemistry, r/Physics, and nanoscience-focused discussion communities for broader discussion threads (this is a niche enough field that dedicated communities are limited; conference proceedings and journal literature are generally more productive sources).
- ArXiv's cond-mat category for recent preprints in this space.

## Companion Repos (adjacent, complementary content)
- **Biomimetic Structural Engineer** — relevant background on computational design/simulation validation discipline and honest technology-readiness assessment, applied in a different but analogous engineering context.
- **Synthetic Biology Designer** and **Genomics Data Scientist** — relevant if pursuing the biomolecular electronics intersection discussed in section 7.

## Staying Current
- This field moves quickly at the research frontier — recent journal publications and preprints are far more reliable sources of current state-of-the-art conductance values, techniques, and technology readiness than any static reference, including this repo.
- Be prepared for genuine, ongoing debate in this field about which specific molecular design strategies, computational methods, or application directions represent the most promising path forward — this repo aims to model appropriately careful, technically grounded skepticism and honest technology-readiness assessment rather than advocate for any single research group's specific approach.
